About Us

Our mission is to give you the opportunities to plan the Tanitian adventure of your dreams. Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture

Who We Are

We are a team of seasoned travelers who have collectively spent years exploring the islands of French Polynesia. We've sailed across turquoise waters, hiked through lush jungles, savored mouthwatering local cuisine, and immersed ourselves in the rich tapestry of Tanitian culture. Our firsthand experiences and deep connections within the region have allowed us to curate a treasure trove of insider knowledge that we can't wait to share with you.

FAQ

A: Power outlets are 120 volts

A: Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

A: The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not stricly enforced.

A: Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

A: There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

A: Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spken in rural areas,especially by the older residents.

A: Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

A: Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
